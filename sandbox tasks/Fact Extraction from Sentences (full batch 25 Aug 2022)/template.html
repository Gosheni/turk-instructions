Can the fact be inferred from the sentence? (~ 5 mins)

General guidelines (all relationships)

Determine whether the suggested fact can be inferred from the sentence alone. A suggested fact consists of a relationship between the two highlighted entities (i.e. things or values) in the sentence. This task asks whether the suggested fact can be inferred from the sentence, it does not matter if the suggested fact is factually correct or outdated or not. Also, it does not matter which mention of the entity is highlighted (when it appears multiple times). If the suggested fact cannot be reliably inferred from the sentence alone (the sentence is hypothetical or a quotation) then mark it as not inferred.

Relationship-specific guidelines

The "is a track work by" relation is used to denote songs or pieces of music created by a musician or band. Note that the sentence should contain the information that the band wrote/released the piece of music, and not just performed it.


Annotate as inferred any sentences from which you can extract the musician or band which created a song. For example, the following sentences should be annotated as inferred:

     "Halo is a single by Beyonce"

     "J.S.Bach’s Goldberg Variationswere first published in 1741 as an Aria and 30 Variations.

     "In 2016, Beyonce performed her single Halo"

In the last example, because the text says "Beyonce performed her single" implies that Beyonce wrote the song Halo, we can annotate it as inferred.


Annotate as not inferred any sentences where it is unclear whether the musician/band created the piece of music. For example, the following sentences should be annotated as not inferred:

     "In 2016, Beyonce performed Halo"

In this example, because it is unclear from the text if Beyonce wrote the song Halo, or just performed it, you should annotate it as not inferred.
