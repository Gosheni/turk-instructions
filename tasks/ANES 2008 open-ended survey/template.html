Survey Instructions


This survey has 420 questions about how you feel about politics and the upcoming U.S. election, including 15 short essay answers. The survey should take 1-1.5 hours to complete.

  • For the short essay answers, please write a few sentences or a whole paragraph for each one. The longer the better. The answers should be in your own words and express your own opinion, so don't copy and paste from anywhere.
  • Please answer all of the multiple choice questions and at least 10 of the short essays as you can.
  • Some of the questions are redundant - we use those to check that people are paying attention and their answers are consistent.
  • To check whether people are clicking randomly, we include a few questions with a correct (and obvious) answer. We will reject people who get them wrong. Don't worry - if you're paying attention you'll definitely get them right.

If you already did this survey, please do not submit it again! There will be another chance for you to answer it again after the election.

  
Informed Consent Form

Purpose of research study: We are collecting opinions about politics and the US election along with demographic information. The information will be used to conduct sociological and linguistic research, including computational linguistics research based on your short essay responses.

Benefits: Although it will not directly benefit you, this study may benefit society by leading to better survey techniques for future elections or by improving the ways that computers process human languages.

Risks:There are no risks for participating in this study.

Voluntary participation:You may stop participating at any time without penalty by clicking on the “Return HIT” button, or closing your browser window.

We may end your participation if you do not follow the instructions, your answers to the controls questions deviate from the expected answers, your are not in the USA or if you submit the survey multiple times from different accounts.

Confidentiality: The only identifying information kept about you will be a WorkerID serial number and your IP address. This information may be disclosed to other researchers.

Questions/concerns: You may e-mail questions to the principle investigator, Chris Callison-Burch. If you feel you have been treated unfairly you may contact the Johns Hopkins University Institutional Review Board.

Clicking on the “Accept HIT” button indicates that you understand the information in this consent form. You have not waived any legal rights you otherwise would have as a participant in a research study.
