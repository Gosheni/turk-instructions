Instructions

Read a sentence fragment and label what you think people might do before and after this event.

Full Instructions     (Expand/Collapse)

Thanks for participating in this HIT!

You will read sentence fragments depicting events. The names of specific people have been replaced by generic words (e.g. "PersonX", "PersonY").

The fragment may also be a figurative expression (e.g. "PersonX cuts corners"). If the fragment is figurative, please answer all questions according to the figurative meaning.

If the event does not make sense and you think there was a typo, please correct it in the Optional Feedback box.

Please note:

We have automatically prefiltered these events to try to choose ones that are understandable. Therefore, our expectation is that you will be able to fill in some responses for most of these events. If you are struggling to come up with answers for several HITs in a row, you may want to contact us to clarify the instructions.

 

Disclaimer for UW affiliates:

Current employees of the University of Washington, family members of UW employees, and UW students involved in this particular research are not eligible to complete this HIT.

 

For each event, think about:

  • What someone might typically need to do before this event
  • What someone might typically want to do next after this event
 

Below are examples:

PersonX mows the lawn PersonX shakes PersonY awake

1. Does PersonX typically need to do anything before this event?

Yes (mowing the lawn requires specific events happen first)

a). What are does PersonX need to do?

- get the lawn mower out (because the lawn mower is necessary in doing the event);
- turn the lawn mower on (if the lawn mower is off the event cannot be completed)

1. Does PersonX typically need to do anything before this event?

Yes (shaking someone requires certain actions first)

a). What are does PersonX need to do?

- find PersonY (in order to shake PersonY, PersonX needs to know where PersonY is);
- grab PersonY (shaking requires that PersonX physically touch PersonY)

2. What will PersonX likely want to do next, after this event?

- drink a glass of water (mowing the lawn is usually exercise which makes one want water);
- take a shower (mowing the lawn usually makes one sweaty);
- rest (mowing the lawn is usually tiring)

2. What will PersonX likely want to do next, after this event?

- tell PersonY something (telling someone something is a common reason for waking them up);
- to go somewhere (going somewhere is another common reason for waking someone up)

3. Does this event affect people other than PersonX?

No (there are no other people involved in the event)

a). What do they likely want to do next, after this event?

N/A

3. Does this event affect people other than PersonX?

Yes (PersonY is explicitly stated)

a). What do they likely want to do next, after this event?

- to snap at PersonX (being woken abruptly often makes people grumpy);
- to go back to sleep (people abruptly woken up are often still tired and want to continue sleeping)
