Find Important Sentences That Should be Included in a Meeting Report

Note: DO NOT DO THIS TASK if you have worked on this particular meeting before. (Check the sentences in the table under "The sentences to label" to make sure.) If you do this task again, you may not get paid!

Instructions:

You will be shown sentences that people spoke in a meeting. Someone else (not you!) needs to write a report on the meeting. Your job is to find important sentences that should be included in the report, unimportant sentences that should not be included in the report, and neutral sentences that the report-writer may or may not include in the report.

In the table below, do the following for each row:

  • Read the sentence in the row.
  • If you think that the sentence should be included in the report - click on the circle next to the word important in the row.
  • If you think that the sentence should not be included in the report - click on the circle next to the word unimportant.
  • If you think that the sentence is of medium importance and may or may not need to be in the report - click on the circle next to the word neutral.

Examples (these are just examples, there may be other sentences that don't fit these types):

  • Examples of important sentences:
    • A sentence in which someone is reporting on work done / progress since last week, or the outcome of something he's done.
    • A sentence in which someone is talking of a problem.
    • A sentence in which someone is discussing a future plan of action.
    • A sentence that summarizes a long discussion.
  • Example of neutral sentences:
    • Sentences describing something in minute details. If there is a sentence that has a summary of the details, that sentence is likely to be important, and the sentences with the minute details are likely to be neutral.
  • Examples of unimportant sentences:
    • A sentence where someone is telling a joke that has little to do with the rest of the meeting.
    • Sentences that are about the dynamics of the meeting, e.g. "Let's go on to the next agenda item."

Tips to improve your work:

  • If some parts of a long sentence seem important and other parts don't, label the sentence as important.
  • On the other hand, if a sentence seems to have been broken into two or three lines, you can label each of those lines differently if you want.
  • Generally in these meetings, about a quarter of the sentences are important, a quarter are neutral and half are unimportant.
