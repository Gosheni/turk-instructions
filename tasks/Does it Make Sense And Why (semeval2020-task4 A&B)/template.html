Write a total of five sentences.

The first should be a correct statement;

The second one which does not make sense, using a similar sentence structure as the first.

Thirdly the right reason why the incorrect statement does not make common sense,

Finally provide two confusing reasons to confuse the machine.

There are several rules you should follow when you write samples:

1. Each confusing reason should contain all common important information in the incorrect statement and the right reason. For example, in the good example1, "elephant" and "fridge" are the important information since both of them appear in the incorrect statement and the right reason. So, the two confusing reasons should contain 'elephant' and 'fridge'

2. The confusing reasons should be correct themselves. See at the bad example 2.

3. If some information only appear at 'correct statement' but not 'incorrect statement' or 'right reason', please don't mention the information at the two confusing reasons. For example, in the good example1, the confusing reasons should not mention "turkey", since the incorrect statement and the right reason don't mention 'turkey'.

4. The right and confusing reasons should be in similar length.

5. Make the questions easy to understand.

6. Avoid using specific knowledge, like persons, places, history.

7. The right reason should not be just like the correct statement.

8. Please don't simply add a 'not' to the incorrect statement to form the reason.

9. Please don't imitate the "elephant" and "fridge" example. We will reject all "XX cannot fit in YY" or "XX is bigger/smaller than YY" answers.

Common Mistakes (Examples can be viewed below)

You should not use knowledge, like specific persons, places, history, to write statements.

The confusing reasons should not lack some important information from the incorrect statement and the right reason.

You should not try to find specific case where the statement is true

Don't make the right reasons be always too short or too long among the three reasons.

The reasons should not mention correct statements.

The confusing reasons should not be wrong themselves.
