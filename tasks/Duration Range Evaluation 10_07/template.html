TASK DESCRIPTION:

In this task we assess the duration of an action or a situation.
Tell us if the duration specified is reasonable for the given action/situation.

  • Reasonable: The durations are reasonable for the action/situation. [Example 1, Example 2]
  • Moderately Reasonable: The durations are moderately reasonable. Or the durations are reasonable for the action/situation once variabilities of an action/situation is considered. [Example 3, Example 4, Example 5]
  • Unreasonable: The durations are not reasonable. [Example 6]
  • Updated!Cannot Interpret Action/Situation: Cannot interpret what the action or the situation means, or the action/situation contains a personal name (of an individual or a particular entity like a company). Names of locations are not included here because they persist over time and across individuals. [Example 7]

TIP #1 If you find yourself stuck between Reasonable and Moderately Reasonable, please go with your gut/intuition.

TIP #2 If you simply can't interpret the action/situation or significant amount of information is missing from the phrase, it is okay to mark Cannot Interpret Action/Situation. We are very much aware that there are certain levels of odd phrases in the data!

HOW TO READ THE CHART:
  • Y-axis: Duration unit categories (seconds, minutes, hours, decades, etc)
  • X-axis: Probability distribution in percentage (%) over the Y-axis categories. Given Example 4 above,
    • "hold" has ~27% chance of lasting seconds
    • "hold" has ~26% chance of lasting years
    • "hold" has ~11% chance of lasting decades
    • "hold" has ~2% chance of lasting hours
    In other words, the act of "holding" will mostly likely last seconds or years.
  • A good chart will reflect highest % in the most relevant duration units and less in others

TIP #3 Overall, you want to pay closest attention to the peaks (longest/longer bars). If those seem to be on target, you can be more forgiving of the low percentage point bars (especially ones that are have very low likelihood).

TIP #4 This data will be used to teach AI systems. Do what you can to be accurate, but also trust your instincts and don't stress over a single question for too long.

FAQs:
  • This action/situation doesn't seem to have a duration. How do I judge this?
    Construct a sentence by adding on "for X minutes/hours" or "in X minutes/hours".
    • put beer in fridge
      - "I put the beer in the fridge for 30 minutes": It means you kept on putting in bottles of beer in the fridge.
    • discovered
      - "They discovered the cure in 10 days!": It means it took them 10 days to get to the point of discovery.
    These are valid interpretations. Now check if the given durations make sense under the interpretation.
  • New! I can think of a potential duration that is missing from the chart. How do I handle this?
    Ask yourself: if the missing duration bar were to be present in the chart, would it be among the longest peaks (or longer than the longest peaks)?
    • If yes, this means a significant loss of information in the chart, so the data would be Unreasonable.
    • If no (the bar would be relatively short in the chart), then the data is likely Moderately Reasonable.
