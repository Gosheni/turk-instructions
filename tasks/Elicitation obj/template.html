What can this action be done to?

  • Watch a short clip from YouTube of an actor performing an action (requires Flash).
  • First, give a word that describes what is happening in the video.  Don't be too specific for the action; if you want to use more than one word, you're being too specific.
  • Next, in the blanks below the video, supply at least one or two things, people, or animals that the action can be done to.  Try to be specific and creative for the tings the action can be done to; writing "man" or "thing" for everything will get your responses rejected.
  • As you give your responses, a text box will fill in with an example sentence with your action.  It's okay if the grammar doesn't work out correctly; it's just there to guide you.
  • In order to make sure that we get good data, you will need to match the answer of at least one other participant in order to get credit for this HIT.  So, it is in your interest to to give more answers rather than fewer and to try to "match" what other people would say.  (If you've ever watched Family Feud, it's like that.)
  • For example, if you saw a movie of someone dunking a basketball, you would write "dunking" as the action and then "ball", "cookie", "basketball", "cracker", etc. for what can be "dunked".
  • It's also possible that there is nothing that the action can be done to (for example, you can't say "it is sleeping him"); if that is the case, click on the check box that says "there is nothing this action can be done to" instead of writing in the boxes.
  • HINT: You can use "TAB" to move between text boxes without clicking.
