We are performing a research study on writing style. Please read the following messages or fragments of messages and determine the formality of each message. In general, we consider formal language to the type of language that is appropriate for professional and business conmmunication. However, there is no perfect definition of formality, so use your own experience and best judgement to make your decisions, keeping in mind the following guidelines:

  • The formality of an email should not necessarily be dictated by the relationship between the sender and the recipient. I.e. it is possible for an employee to send an informal message to their boss. 
  • The formality of an email should not necessarily be dictated by the personal/business nature of the email. I.e. it is possible for a person to send an informal business message and to send a formal personal message.
  • Please consider only the text of the primary message show. E.g. if an email contains text of forwarded emails or attachments, do not consider them when making your judgement. 
  • If the email is blank, not in English, or otherwise uninterpretable, please choose I cannot tell.

Also, please indicate using the check box if you believe the message was not part of a person-to-person communication, e.g. if it appears to be spam or promotional. You should still rate the formality of the text, even if you check this checkbox. 

These HITs will be quality controlled. If you are not sure whether you are doing them correctly, please do a small number and wait for feedback before doing more. Thank you in advance!

 

Examples of very formal messages (should receive a rating of 6 or 7):

I am writing this letter in hopes that you may be able to help me.  My name is Lynde M. Mongiovi. All my life, I have worked unbelievably hard to fulfill my dream of becoming a teacher in the Exceptional Education field...

Thank you for your consideration of our request. We hope to hear from you at your earliest convenience so we can begin planning your visit. We appreciate all you are doing for Florida and our country .

I would like to thank you for responding to my e-mail as I seriously doubted that you would. You were right when you said I should be informed before I make such comments as I did .   However , you were wrong to assume that I wasn't informed to begin with. As a Co-Chair of the Kids Voting Broward Advisory Board...I have done extensive research on many of the laws in Florida as well as many of the programs you've initiated since taking office last year....
