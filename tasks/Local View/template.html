Compare two dictionary definitions

Instructions

Your task is to indicate if the two definitions for a given word concern the same meaning or different meanings.

Dictionaries will give many definitions for a word, where many of these definitions concern the same general meaning, but detail different specific aspects of that meaning. To illustrate what we mean by general and specific, here is a quick example.  The word "bark" might have 6 definitions in the dictionary, but there are really only two general meanings, one for the 'sound of a dog' and one for the 'protective covering of a plant'.  One of the 6 definitions might mention that "bark" means : speak in an unfriendly tone.  That is just a specific case of the 'sound of a dog' transferring to human use.

  Two definitions have the same meaning if they refer to the same general meaning.
  Two definitions have different meanings if they refer to different general meanings.
