Indicate how many general meanings there are in a list of dictionary definitions

Instructions

 

 Your task is to indicate how many general meanings there are in a list of dictionary definitions. 

Dictionaries will give many definitions for the same word, where many of these definitions concern the same general meaning, but detail different specific aspects of that meaning.  We are looking for the number of general meanings in a list of definitions.  

To illustrate what we mean by general and specific, here is a quick example.  The word bark might have 6 definitions in the dictionary, but there are really only two general meanings, one for the 'sound of a dog' and one for the 'protective covering of a plant'.  One of the 6 definitions might mention that bark means : speak in an unfriendly tone.  That is just a specific case of the 'sound of a dog' transferring to human use.

Below are more examples.

Examples

Word: Anticipate

Definitions:

  • to do something before someone else
  • to expect that something will happen and be ready for it
  • to imagine or expect that something will happen, sometimes taking action in preparation for it happening
  • to think about something that is going to happen, especially something pleasant
  • to use or consider something before you should

Answer: Here there is only 1 general meaning. Remember, we do not care about specific meanings.

 

Word: Abstract

Definitions:

  • a painting, design etc which contains shapes or images that do not look like real things or people
  • a painting which represents the qualities of something, not its outer appearance
  • a shortened form of a speech, article, book, etc., giving only the most important facts or arguments
  • a short written statement of the most important ideas in a speech, article etc
  • considered in a general way rather than being based on specific details and examples

Answer: Here there are 3 general meanings, one for the painting, one for writing/speaking and one for the abstract object

 

Word: Clarify

Definitions:

  • to make something clearer and easier to understand
  • to make something clear or easier to understand by giving more details or a simpler explanation
  • to remove water and unwanted substances from fat, such as butter, by heating it

Answer: Here there are 2 general meanings.

 

Word: Depress

Definitions:

  • to cause someone to feel unhappy and without hope for the future
  • to lower the level or amount of something
  • to make someone feel very unhappy
  • to press down or lower
  • to press something down, especially a part of a machine
  • to prevent something from working properly or being as active as it usually is
  • to reduce the amount of activity in something, especially a business operation
  • to reduce the value of prices or wages
  • to reduce the value of something, especially money

Answer : Here there are 2 general meanings, one for 'causing someone to feel unhappy' and one for reducing either 'values/prices or machine parts'.
