Considerations:

When determining the lables for the paragraphs, be careful about the difference between facts and sentiments. If someone just stating the negative/positive facts about the entity the paragraph might not be negative/positive. If someone is using the facts to express an opinion towards the entity, probably that paragraph is negative/positive. Followings are two examples of such situations:

  • An example of a negative fact towards Bill Clinton which is not supported by the author so the paragraph will be neutral: Journalist Jen Moore died under suspicious circumstances after contacting both the DHS and the FBI with allegations that former President Bill Clinton committed sexual assault.

  • An example of a negative fact about Bill Clinton which is used by the author to express negative opinion hence makes the paragraph negative: In the movie Ocean’s Eleven, there’s a great scene where Julia Roberts’ character accuses her ex-husband, played by George Clooney, of being a thief and a liar. He retorts, “I only lied about being a thief.” Even if Bill Clinton only lied about sex, it was the lie (more than the sex) that did him in.

 

Tips about Quotes

Be careful when dealing with quotes. If an article simply quotes someone talking negative about ${entity}, that does not mean the article is negative towards ${entity}, in this case it would be neutral. If however the article cherry-picks quotes or uses quotes strategically to support its view then this would not be neutral. Here are some examples about different quotes:
  • An example of a negative quote which is used by the author to support the negative view towards Obama: Congress must act because President Obama has been completely feckless in his entire approach to Cuba. He received nothing in exchange for many substantial concessions made to the Communist regime. After five years of “negotiations” President Obama ended exactly where Raul Castro’s demands began five years ago. As Senator Marco Rubio said, President “Obama is the worst negotiator since Carter.”
  • An example of a negative quote which is not supported by the author hence makes the paragraph neutral towards Obama: Republican critics say Obama is making too many concessions to Cuba for too little in return, especially on human rights issues. “After two years of President Obama’s Cuba policy, the Castro regime has made out like bandits,” said U.S. Senator Marco Rubio, a Cuban-American lawmaker from Florida.

Other Tips
