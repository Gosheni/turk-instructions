Sort adjectives along a scale

You will be shown a list of adjectives which refer to the same attribute (e.g. "warm", "hot" and "scalding" all describe temperature). Please drag and drop to sort the adjectives from the weakest/most neutral adjective to the strongest/most intense adjective. 

If multiple adjectives are of equal strength, and thus cannot be ordered, use the yellow lines to designate groups of equally weighted adjectives (see the third and forth examples below).

Occassionally, there may be adjectives in the group that seem to describe a slightly different attribute than the others. Please do your best to order them anyway, you will not be punished for your choices on where to rank misplaced adjectives.

If all adjectives are of the same intensity, it is fine to have them all in a single group. However, only do this if absolutely necessary. Please try to place them in some order whenever possible. 

Some examples of correct orderings are below.

warm < hot < scalding

Note that no yellow delimiters are needed here since

there are no "ties" in the strength of the words.

 attractive < pretty < beautiful < drop-dead gorgeous

big = large < huge < enormous = gigantic

Use the yellow delimiters to indicate that "big" and "large"

are of equal strength, as are "enormous" and "gigantic." 

You can place extra delimiters at the top or the bottom of the list.

loud < noisy < ear-splitting = deafening
