Instructions

It is normal to simplify our choice of words when speaking to children or to non-native English speakers. For example, the word 'children' is simpler than the word 'progeny'. Please indicate, in your opinion, which phrase in each pair is simpler. Try to think of the words as paraphrases of one another, and consider the most common contexts in which the phrase is spoken or written when making your decision.

Afterwards, please indicate, on a scale from 1 to 5, how similar the two words are in meaning, where 5 means "very similar" and 1 means "not at all similar."

Thank you in advance! 

Important Note: The words and phrases have been stemmed, meaning they are in their "base form" (e.g. verbs may not be conjugated, nouns may not be plural). For example, words like "is" and "was" will be written as "be". Therefore, some phrases might appear strange or ungrammatical. Look at the examples below to familiarize yourself with what stemmed phrases look like. Please DO NOT let these types of non-grammaticalities affect your judgements of the complexity of the phrases.

Examples of what "stemmed" phrases might look like (original phrase -> stemmed phrase):

was called into -> be call into

said yesterday -> say yesterday

pretty excited -> pretty excite
