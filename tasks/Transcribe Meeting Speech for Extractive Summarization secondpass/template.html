TASK: Correct a Transcription

Instructions:

  1. In this HIT you will check and possibly correct a transcription. Accuracy is crucial. The transcription may require one or both of the following correcitons:
    1. Missing parts: Portions of the speech that were not transcribed. You should listen to that part of the recording and fill in the transcription.
    2. Mistakes: Sometimes the available transcription is incorrect (e.g., wrong words, incorrect or missing annotations). You should correct these as well.

    For example:
    • Correct:
      i agree that ___ is addressed
    • To:
      i ?agrue? (agree) that the issue is addressed

  2. Listen to the audio below by pressing the "Play" button. You may replay this audio as many times as you'd like, especially while you're correcting and adding to the transcriptions below. The audio should be no longer than N seconds in length.
  3. Answer the questions below.

Transcription Guidelines:

  • True case is used.  All words are lower case, except for proper nouns, which begin with a capital letter.
  • Please do not include any punctuation (periods, commas, etc.) in your transcription except for apostrophes where appropriate (like "let's").
  • Please write out all numbers as whole words. E.g., forty five, ninety
  • Transcribe mispronunciations and false starts correctly. (See below for more details.)
  • Transcribe speech fillers as follows:
    • uh for "uh"
    • um for "um"
    • lg for laughter

Mispronunciations and False Starts:

These may or may not occur in the particular speech that you are being assigned to transcribe. If they do, please follow these guidelines:

  • Mispronunciations (including word fragments): This is when someone mispronounces a word. The first portion of text is the speaker’s actual pronunciation, the parenthesized text is your best guess as to what was intended. Put question marks around the mispronunciation, right after you type in what you believe the person intended to say, like this:

    • ... ?uttered? (intended) ...

    Examples:
    • came on ?mongey? (monday) afternoon
    • arrive by ?sev? (seven) p m

      •
  • False starts: This is when the speaker makes a correction by starting again. Place angle brackets around the phrase that was repeated, like this:

    • < ... >

    Example:
    • then <turn left> no i'm sorry turn right
