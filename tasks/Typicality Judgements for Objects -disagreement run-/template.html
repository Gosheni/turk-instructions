Thanks for participating in this HIT!

In this HIT, given an entity and a function, we will ask you to:

  • decide the categorical type of the entity:
    • object: this is an thing, object, or item (MOST COMMON).
    • person: this is a person or human.
    • living thing: this is a non-human living entity like animals and plants.
    • place: this is a place or a location.
    • action: this is an action, situation or event.
    • body part: this is an external part or internal part of body.
    • other: none of the above.
    • invalid: no idea what this is.
  • make a snap judgement about typicality or expectedness of the function/role given the entity
    • highly expected: the statement describes an absolutely normal or usual.
    • somewhat expected: the statement is somewhat normal or usual.
    • somewhat unusual: the statement is somewhat unusual or creative.
    • highly unusual: the statement is highly unusual or rather creative.
    • weird or invalid: the statement is invalid, weird, or nonsensical.
Notes:
  • Sometimes an entity can have multiple meanings (see Examples 1 and 2).
    A "bat", for example, can refer to a baseball bat or the nocturnal animal.
    In such cases, assume that the entity means what best fits the function/role
  • You will invariably encounter some spelling errors. If you can figure out what it means, please do!
    If it is completely undecipherable, mark it as invalid.
 
Examples:
1: bat to/for
play baseball
 
2: bat to/for
watch them fly
 
3: mixing bowl to/for
cover head from rain
 
4: kitten to/for
put inside a cage
 
5: walking to/for
go grocery shopping
 

We want your snap judgement.
